<p><strong>前言</strong>&nbsp;</p>
<p>历史是时光的沉淀，报刊杂志的出版，是历史的文献资料的纪录。&nbsp;不要错以为报刊杂志不是官方出版的，就没有价值。&nbsp;
<br>&nbsp;
<br>一个时代，一个国家，甚至一个社会的发展，孰不知也可通过各种姿彩缤纷的报刊杂志，反映各个时期人民的生活和感受，让我们可以从不同的视角，不同的层面，不同的阐析，了解历史的意义和价值。有了这个基石，可以启发和激励一代一代的人去创造，去发挥生活更深更广的内涵，再创更有意义和更有价值的历史。&nbsp;
<br>&nbsp;
<br>文献资料的保存是非常重要的工作，而偏偏这样的工作面临种种困难。&nbsp;
<br>&nbsp;
<br>许多报刊杂志或公藏或私藏，往往都不齐全。除个别的一些因素之外，主要还是收藏不当。不是蛀蚀了，就是受潮了；加上空间的局限，不是卖了，就是丢了，幸运的是送给了另一个知音去收藏，这是很无可奈何的事情。&nbsp;
<br>&nbsp;
<br>当我们着手整理和出版这本书时，就面对了期刊收存不齐的问题，让我们感到惭愧和遗憾。&nbsp;
<br>&nbsp;
<br>在机缘巧合之下，我馆和海南会馆读书会都有共同意愿，要即时把这份工作做起来，为我们国家保存更完整的文献资料。我们于2007年10月间联手举办了为期一个月的“新加坡华文期刊50年”展。这个展出得到多位私藏家的大力支持,
或惠借或捐赠，期刊展深受好评。</p>

### **新加坡华文期刊50年**


<p><strong>总介</strong>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>作者：韩山元</strong>&nbsp;</p>
<p>从上世纪40年代到80年代，是个不平凡的大时代，新加坡社会经历了一波又一波的巨变，人民也经历了一轮又一轮的考验。从英国统治到日本统治，再回到英国统治，然后是争取独立，内部自治，加入马来西亚，最后又退出马国成立独立的共和国。&nbsp;
<br>&nbsp;
<br>不平凡的时代总会留下记录，记录这个时代的出版物会因此变得不平凡。&nbsp;
<br>&nbsp;
<br>本书收录的从1940年到1980年代的华文期刊，就是半个世纪以来新加坡经历天翻地覆变化的缩影。前辈们可以从中找回往年的记忆，年轻人可以看到前辈留下的深深脚印。&nbsp;
<br>&nbsp;
<br>让我们借此形成不分老中青的共同记忆，也让我们体会文化拓荒者创业的艰辛。&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>&nbsp;</strong>
</p>
<p><strong>战争与和平 ――20世纪40年代的期刊</strong>&nbsp;</p>
<p>上世纪40年代，新加坡经历了有史以来最严酷的考验与战争的浩劫，也经历了战后重建的艰辛与政局动荡的折腾。所有这些，都在当时出版的期刊中留下珍贵的记录。&nbsp;
<br>&nbsp;
<br>战前本地的文化阵地主要是报章的副刊，期刊非常少。&nbsp;
<br>&nbsp;
<br>战后是期刊逐渐走向繁荣的年代，胡愈之主编的《风下》周刊与许云樵主编的《南洋杂志》都是当时影响很大的期刊。此外，还有《人民周刊》、《新妇女》、《星期六》等。&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>&nbsp;</strong>
</p>
<p><strong>火红的年代 ――50年代期刊姿采纷呈</strong>&nbsp;</p>
<p>进入50年代，新加坡华文期刊迎来了空前繁荣的时期，文艺刊物、事时杂志、娱乐画报、儿童期刊，姿采纷呈，洋洋大观。&nbsp;
<br>&nbsp;
<br>姚紫主编的《文艺行列》于1950年创刊，紧接着星洲日报推出《星洲周刊》，南洋商报推出《南洋月报》。此外还有《世界少年》、《世界儿童》》等等。&nbsp;
<br>&nbsp;
<br>50年代的新加坡政局动荡，工人运动、学生运动风起云涌，波澜壮阔，那是战后华校生格外活跃的年代，他们不让青春留白，锲而不舍地创办了一个又一个影响很大的杂志，如《荒地》、《耕耘》、《人间》、《时代报》、《文艺报》、《行动周报》等。此外，《生活文丛》、《人闻》则是当时影响较大的本地文艺期刊。当新马还是一家时，颇有影响力的《蕉风月刊》先在新加坡出版，后移到吉隆坡继续出版。&nbsp;
<br>&nbsp;
<br>南洋大学创校之后，南大学生出版物琳琅满目，给50年代的期刊出版锦上添花。&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>&nbsp;</strong>
</p>
<p><strong>激进与温和 ――60年代期刊风格迥异</strong>&nbsp;</p>
<p>60年代的华文期刊在1966年之前基本上延续50年代的路线，1966年之后受中国文化大革命的影响，出现一些内容激进的刊物，如左派政党社会主义阵线的《新青年》，此外，还有《建设》、《文艺生活》等，新加坡大学中文学会与新加坡工艺学院中文协会也都出版了自己的刊物。&nbsp;
<br>&nbsp;
<br>与内容激进的期刊迥然不同的文艺刊物《猎户》的出现，给文坛带来一股新风。&nbsp;
<br>&nbsp;
<br>当时影响较大的期刊还有《石叻周报》、《新社文艺》、《新诗月报》等。&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>&nbsp;</strong>
</p>
<p><strong>沙漠有绿洲 ――文风逐渐疲弱的70至80年代</strong>&nbsp;</p>
<p>从70年代到80年代，新加坡的语文环境有较大的变化，尤其在80年代后半期，文风趋于疲弱，华文期刊市场萎缩，然而，仍有一群有心人逆风而行，顽强地抗拒“荒漠化”，艰辛地维持一块又一块小小的绿洲。&nbsp;
<br>&nbsp;
<br>这个期间的《奔流》、《知识天地》、《笔端》、《赤道诗刊》、《乡城文艺》、《蜗牛》、《同温层》都是有影响力的期刊。新加坡大学中文学会先后出版了《激风》、《绿洲》，南大中国语文学会出版了《新生》，新加坡工艺学院中文协会推出了《新时代》，基本上维持了70年代初到中期的盛况。&nbsp;
<br>&nbsp;
<br>进入80年代后半期，华文期刊告别了辉煌和灿烂。但是，仍有《赤道风》、《海峡诗刊》、《热带文艺》、《天空》等期刊坚强地发出微弱的声音。&nbsp;
<br>&nbsp;
<br>由此可见，沙漠中仍有绿洲，绿洲仍有浅浅的清流，清流仍滋养着生命力顽强的仙人掌
</p>

### **50 years of Singapore Chinese Periodicals**

<p><strong>Introduction</strong>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Translation of “ 新加坡华文期刊50年” written by: Han Tan Juan (韩山元)</strong>&nbsp;
<br>&nbsp;
<br>The period between the 1940s and 1980s was an extraordinary period in
the history of Singapore. The country experienced many major changes, from
a British colony to falling under the Japanese Occupation during World
War II and thereafter, a return to British rule. This was followed by the
struggle for independence, political unrest, and a brief merger with Malaysia.
Finally came the separation of Singapore from Malaysia to become a sovereign,
democratic and independent nation in 1965. Life was tumultuous but our
forefathers braved those turbulent times. Matching this period of great
changes were publications that were extraordinary.&nbsp;
<br>&nbsp;
<br>The periodicals displayed in this publication were published between 1940s
and 1980s. They represent the changes Singapore had undergone. Showcasing
this collection of local Chinese periodicals offers an opportunity for
the older generation to reminisce about the past, and for the younger generation
to catch a glimpse of the trials and tribulations experienced by our forefathers.&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>&nbsp;</strong>
</p>
<p><strong>War And Peace -- Periodicals published in the 1940s</strong>&nbsp;</p>
<p>The 1940s were some of the harshest times in the history of Singapore.
World War II raged and caused unparalleled trials and massive rebuilding
efforts in its aftermath. The political situation was restless and this
was evident in the periodicals published in the 1940s.&nbsp;
<br>&nbsp;
<br>The pre-war period saw very few periodicals published. Newspaper supplements,
(副刊fu kan), had the greatest literary presence then. After World War II,
the publications of periodicals flourished. The two most influential periodicals
during this period were 《风下》(feng xia) and 《南洋杂志》(The Nanyang Miscellany).
The chief editors of these periodicals were 胡愈之(Hu Yuzhi) and 许云樵 (Xu Yunqiao)
respectively. Other periodicals published during this period included 《人民周刊》(Renmin
zhou kan),《新妇女》(Xin fu nǚ’) and 《星期六》(Saturday Review).&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>&nbsp;</strong>
</p>
<p><strong>The Fiery Decade -- Periodicals published in the 1950s</strong>&nbsp;</p>
<p>The Chinese publications industry of the 1950s was vibrant and energetic.
Chinese periodicals flourished in an array of styles and content. They
ranged from art and entertainment to current affairs and literature. 《文艺行列》
(Wen yi hang lie) edited by 姚紫 (Yao Zi), started publication in 1950. This
was followed closely by 《星洲周刊》(Sin Chew Weekly)and ‘《南洋月报》(Nan yang Monthly’).
Other publications included 《世界少年》(Shi jie shao nian) and 《世界儿童》 (Shi jie
er tong’).&nbsp;
<br>&nbsp;
<br>The 1950s was a time of political unrest. Workers’ strikes and student
demonstrations took to the streets frequently. Students from the Chinese
schools were very active during this era, launching many influential magazines
such as《荒地》(Huang di), 《耕耘》(Geng yun’), 《人间》 (Ren jian’),《时代报》 (Shi dai
bao),《文艺报》 (Wen yi bao) and《行动周刊》 (Petir Weekly) that were aimed to influence
the masses. Among them, 《生活文丛》(Sheng huo wen cong) and 《人间》 (Ren jian )
were two of the most important local periodicals of the time. Another important
publication was《蕉风月刊》(Bulanan Chao Foon) which had a large reader base.
It was first published in Singapore. After 1957, its publication office
was moved to Kuala Lumpur.&nbsp;
<br>&nbsp;
<br>With the establishment of the Nanyang University, more periodicals were
published, adding more variety to an already booming industry.&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>&nbsp;</strong>
</p>
<p><strong>Extreme Vs Moderate -- Periodicals published in the 1960s</strong>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Periodicals published before 1966 were very similar to those of the 1950s.
With the start of the Cultural Revolution in China in 1966, some periodicals
with an extremist tone emerged in Singapore. One example is 《新青年》 (Xin
qing nian) which was published by the leftist Barisan Sosialis. Other periodicals
of the same period included《建设》(Jian she) and《文艺生活》(Wen yi sheng huo).
The Chinese Society of the University of Singapore and other institutions
also published their own periodicals.&nbsp;
<br>&nbsp;
<br>The launch of 《猎户》(the Huntsmen), a literary magazine that was a departure
from the extremist viewpoint, gave readers an alternative choice. &nbsp;
<br>&nbsp;
<br>Influential periodicals in the 1960s included《石叻周报》(Silat Weekly), 《新社文艺》(Xin
she wen yi) and 《新诗月报》(Poetry Monthly).&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>&nbsp;</strong>
</p>
<p><strong>An Oasis In the Desert -- The decline in the 1970s and 1980s</strong>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Singapore linguistic environment experienced a shift during the 1970s
and 1980s. The change was evident in the Chinese community in the late
1980’s. As the use of Chinese language began to wane, the market for Chinese
periodicals started to shrink.&nbsp;
<br>&nbsp;
<br>However, there were some publications that persisted and created an oasis
in the adverse environment. Influential periodicals in the 1970s and 1980s
included 《奔流》(Ben Liu), 《知识天地》(Zhi shi tian di), 《笔端》(Bi duan), 《赤道诗刊》(Chi
dao shi kan),《乡城文艺》(Xiang cheng wen yi), 《同温层》(Tong wen ceng). The Chinese
Society of the University of Singapore published 《激风》(Ji feng) and 《绿洲》(Lǚ
zhou). Nanyang University’s Chinese Language and Literary Society published
《新生》(Xin sheng’). The Chinese Language Society of the Singapore Polytechnic
published 《新时代》(Xin shi dai). These, as well as publications from other
institutions, helped maintain a vibrant atmosphere till the first half
of 1980s.&nbsp;
<br>&nbsp;
<br>The mid-1980 saw the beginning of the decline in popularity of Chinese
periodicals. Nonetheless, there were still some notable publications during
this period, such as 《赤道风》(Equatorial Wind), 《海峡诗刊》(Hai xia shi kan), 《热带文艺》(Re
dai wen yi) and 《天空》(Sky).&nbsp;</p>
